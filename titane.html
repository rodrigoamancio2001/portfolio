CONCEPT, WRITTEN ESSAY AND DESIGN
Rodrigo Amâncio
BEYOND GENDER, BEYOND HUMAN is a verbal-visual essay created for the film Titane, by Julia Ducournau. The essay conceptually explores how the representation of a body can be deconstructed and non-canonical. In the film itself, we come across a communion between human and machine, flesh and metal, a disruptive way of celebrating the alliance of fiction with identity, a subversion of gender itself.

Through its layout, format and folding, this editorial object sought to create a confusing, labyrinthine, deconstructed and non-linear reading and handling experience (in line with the concept of the film itself). It established a meditation on gender and identity through gestures and images whose sequence creates an almost addictive rhythm.

(This essay subsequently led to the creation of the project METAGENESIS.)
