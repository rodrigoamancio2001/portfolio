And (Now and Then) the Mirror Cracked is the 2022 finalists’ showcase for the bachelor’s degree in Communication Design at the Faculdade de Belas-Artes of UL. As a team, we saw publications as a space where voices come together. And rather than just presenting finished projects, we aimed to bring together their underlying reflections.

We organised the various projects into a big thematic narrative, and then divided them into several clusters. The showcase proposed to (re)view design through a feminine lens and thereby intersect it with the margins, cinema, and fiction. The goal was to reach a critical reflection capable of opening space in academia and in our work, allowing the feminine gaze to widen our field of vision and seeing it as a transgression of dominant discourses.

The editorial strategy of the book was to place several texts and authors side by side on the pages to create parallel readings. In addition, the students’ projects were combined with longer texts that created a common thread. The website also organised the projects into clusters, with a strategy that focused on overlaps and movement. This was a reference to narratives and filmstrips.

(Despite our efforts managing the resources and communicating with the different departments, it was not possible to publish this project due to scheduling and budget allocation issues.)
